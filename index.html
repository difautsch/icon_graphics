Apartment Building Proposed in Crown Heights Historic District

The apartment building is 181 units, seven stories. The size of the lot 89,000 square feet. The developers haven't yet committed to offering affordable housing.

The Crown Heights North historic district was founded in 2007 by the Crown Heights North historical society. After the Barclay’s Center was announced in 2004 the neighborhood realized the importance of “protec(ing) the area from an onslaught of development’ according to Denise Brown, a founder of the CHN historical society.

The lot is home to the Hebron SDA Bilingual School, a k-8 institution, which sits inside the Methodist Home for the Aged (built in 1889). Hope Street has a history of purchasing property from churches including 520 Clinton Ave. and 74 Adams St.
